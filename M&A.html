Take A Peek Into Last Week's M&A Review

Author: Ashrith Desu

The world of mergers and acquisitions never slows down, and the past few days have brought us two interesting deals worth watching. First up, we've got TerraVest Industries making a strategic play in the tank trailer space, followed by TowneBank's move to expand its banking reach. These transactions highlight how companies are actively positioning themselves for growth—whether by entering new markets or doubling down on existing strengths. Let's dive into the details.

1.TerraVest Industries Acquires L.B.T. For $16 Million

On Friday, April 4th, TerraVest Industries announced their acquisition of L.B.T., a North American manufacturer of tank trailers. LBT was acquired for an EV of approximately $16 million. LBT has built a strong reputation for their high-quality and innovative trailers, and fits perfectly into TerraVest’s family with their desire to grow in the tank trailer business. With the resources TerraVest already has, it positions them well in the tanker market and allows them to deliver greater value to their customers and drive growth.

Acquirer: TerraVest Industries (Vegreville, Canada)
Target: L.B.T. Inc. (Omaha, Nebraska)
Deal Value: $16,000,000
Earnings Per Share (EPS): Not Yet Announced
Announcement Date: April 4th, 2025
Expected Close Date: Not Yet Announced
Acquirer Advisors: Undisclosed
Target Advisors: Undisclosed

Following a string of strategic acquisitions across industries, another notable deal in the financial sector emerged...

2. TowneBank Seeks To Acquire Old Point Financial

Townebank has agreed to acquire Old Point Financial, parent of Old Point National Bank of Phoebus, in a deal valued at approximately $203 million. Old Point shareholders can either take $41 cash, or 1.14 shares of TowneBank common stock for each share they held. While the acquisition is still not final due to regulatory and shareholder approvals still necessary, it is expected to go through officially during the second half of the year. TowneBank will definitely benefit from the acquisition, and even predicts it to lead to a boost in their EPS by approximately 10%.

Acquirer: TowneBank
Target: Old Point Financial
Deal Value: $203,000,000
Earnings Per Share (EPS): 10% Accretion
Announcement Date: Not Final
Expected Close Date: Mid to Late 2025
Acquirer Advisors: Piper Sandler & Co + Wachtell, Lipton, Rosen & Katz (Legal Counsel)
Target Advisors: Keefe, Bruyette & Woods + Troutman Pepper Locke (Legal Counsel)

Summary

  • TerraVest Industries acquired L.B.T, a tank trailer manufacturer, for approximately $16 million, expanding its footprint in the tanker market with a focus on growth and innovation.
  • TowneBank is set to acquire Old Point Financial in a $203 million transaction, offering shareholders a choice between $41 in cash or 1.14 shares of TowneBank stock per share held.
  • The TowneBank–Old Point deal is expected to close in mid-to-late 2025, pending regulatory and shareholder approvals, and is projected to be 10% accretive to TowneBank’s EPS.
  • No EPS impact or closing date has yet been disclosed for the TerraVest–L.B.T. deal, though it was announced on April 4th, 2025.
  • These deals show how companies are making bold moves to fuel future growth, with TerraVest doubling down on tank trailers and TowneBank expanding its banking footprint.
